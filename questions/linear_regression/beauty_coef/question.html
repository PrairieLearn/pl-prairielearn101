In the "beauty in the classroom" case study, when we included all of the "beauty" features in the model,

  • each of the coefficients associated with an individual student's attractiveness ranking - 'bty_f1lower', 'bty_f1upper', 'bty_f2upper', 'bty_m1lower', 'bty_m1upper', 'bty_m2upper' - was approximately 7,
  • while the coefficient associated with the average of the six attractiveness rankings was -42.

 The unusual coefficients are explained by collinearity of these features. When we train the model with only bty_avg and none of the individual scores, the coefficient for bty_avg is a very small positive value, suggesting a very small positive association between physical attractiveness and teaching evaluation scores. When we train the model with only bty_avg and none of the individual scores, the coefficient for bty_avg is still approximately -42. The coefficient for bty_avg is very different from the coefficients for the individual attractiveness ratings because they are measured on different scales. Overall, this suggests that increased physical attractiveness has a negative association with teaching evaluation scores - a more attractive instructor would have lower evaluation scores, on average. These coefficients are a red flag suggesting possible data leakage - the model may be learning from data that would not be available to it at inference time.
