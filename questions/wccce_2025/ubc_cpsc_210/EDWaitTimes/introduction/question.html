In this lab, you will be working on an analysis tool that will help us find some interesting trends in emergency room wait times. In British Columbia, many emergency rooms publish wait time information every 5 minutes on edwaittimes.ca, and we collected a sample of several weeks of this data to analyse. With this data, you will create a tool that will help us explore how wait times are affected by different factors. An example of question we want to answer is "How are wait times affected by the day of the week?" where the factor is the day of the week.

We'll do this by grouping the wait times records by some factor. An example group for day of the week would be "Sunday". We will then compare the averages across all groups by producing a bar chart. So far, we are only interested in seeing how wait times differ by day of the week, but we may want to add more in the future.

List of Responsibilities

In terms of things we need our code to do, we can break it down into a few responsibilities:

  1. Representing all the factors that this tool can group wait times by
    • For example: by day of week, hour of day, etc.
  2. Getting information about factors
    • For example: finding the name/label of a factor (e.g. "Day of week")
  3. Getting all the possible groups for a given factor
    • For example, for our first factor (day of week), all the possible groups are the days of the week: Sunday, Monday, Tuesday, Wednesday, etc.
  4. Finding the group for a given wait time record
  5. Putting a wait time record into its group
  6. Finding the average of these groups of wait times
