This is a sample of a question to use to enable student notetaking. You could also allow students to use their own text editor locally (either on their own computer or on a computer-based testing facility computer, if that's allowed). However, there may be advantages to keeping everything inside PrairieLearn/PrairieTest, e.g., simplifying proctoring instructions. While you could also use the allowPersonalNotes option , that essentially also requires students to use their own editor, since once they enter text, re-opening it is via an external application.

When using this question, we recommend putting it in a zone of its own in the assessment. It must be worth 0 points. (Students cannot earn any marks on this question.)

This question enables:

  • Students can take arbitrary textual notes within an assessment.
  • Students can save (but not grade) and review previous saved versions.
  • Editing continues to be possible until the assessment is closed.
  • Students can review these notes any time they are able to review problems on the assessment.
  • Staff can review these notes any time after they are saved (but note that we explicitly forbade use of these notes in grading in our own course).

Nothing on this "Question" is necessary or graded as part of the exam. No grade above 0% is possible on this "Question".

We provide a textbox below for any scratch notes you want to take during this assessment for your own reference. Nothing in these notes will ever be used in any way in grading.

You can use the Save only button to save your scatch work. The Save & Grade button will not work, however.

If you would like to open the rest of the assesment in a new tab while leaving this page available for access: Hold down Control (Windows) or Command (Mac) and then click the "Assessment overview" button.
