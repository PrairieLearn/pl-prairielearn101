A monoalphabetic cipher is an improvement on the Caesar cipher. It replaces each letter of the alphabet with another letter of the alphabet, based on a translation table. Only letters are converted, spaces and punctuation marks retain their value. The convertion retains case (i.e., lowercase letters are converted to lowercase letters, uppercase letters are converted to uppercase letters).
