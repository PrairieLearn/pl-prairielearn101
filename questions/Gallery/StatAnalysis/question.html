The Caesar cipher is a simple cipher that replaces each letter of the alphabet with another letter of the alphabet, found $k$ elements later, where $k$ is the key for encryption. Only letters are converted, spaces and punctuation marks retain their value. The convertion retains case (i.e., lowercase letters are converted to lowercase letters, uppercase letters are converted to uppercase letters).
